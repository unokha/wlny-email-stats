Hard bad addresses. Includes Undeliverable, Complainer, Spam Trap, Bot, and Malicious results. Continuing to send to these will harm sender reputation and lift bounce rates above the safe threshold.

Recommendation Move to a suppression list. Do not email.

Recommended next steps

A practical sequence to move from the cleaned data to the next campaign.

01

Load the verified file as the active audience

Import the 4,302 verified contacts into the sending platform as the primary list. This becomes the baseline for any campaign that goes out in the next 90 days.

02

Move the suppress bucket out of reach

The 289 hard bad records should be tagged and excluded from every send moving forward. Keeping them in a suppression list rather than deleting them prevents accidental re import later.

03

Park the review bucket for a second pass

The 738 ambiguous addresses are held back and re run through verification after 30 to 60 days. A portion will resolve to verified on the second attempt.

04

Plan a phone or postal touch for high value invalid contacts

The 200 past purchasers with unreachable email are worth a small outbound effort through a different channel. Phone numbers are present on the records.
